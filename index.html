About Us

A Family-Run Restaurant Serving Traditional Madeiran Espetadas

Welcome to the Skewer House – our slice of Madeira in the heart of Taunton. Our family hails from Madeira, a beautiful Portuguese island nestled in the Atlantic Ocean. With deep culinary roots and a passion for hospitality, we’ve brought the island’s rich flavors to Somerset through our signature dish: the Espetada – traditional Madeiran skewers grilled over open flames.


Our Journey

In 2003, our mum opened her first restaurant, O' Picado, in Essex. Specializing in a variety of Portuguese dishes, it quickly gained a loyal following. One dish stood out above all — the Espetada became a customer favorite, loved for its bold seasoning and rustic presentation. When we moved to Somerset in 2014, we saw an opportunity to bring a taste of Portugal to the West Country. We found the perfect setting: a charming listed building in Taunton. With a vision of creating an authentic Casa de Espetada — or “skewer house” — we got to work. We hand-crafted tables using wood from the Quantocks, imported crockery from Madeira, and even repurposed church pews from Bridgwater to give the space a truly unique and welcoming character. Every detail was considered to bring the spirit of Madeira to life. On October 16th, 2016, we proudly opened our doors.

Growing the Family Legacy

Since then, the Skewer House has become one of Taunton’s most loved restaurants, known for our warm hospitality and unforgettable food. Building on that success, we expanded our family venture in September 2019, opening Nobregas Tapas & Paella with our sister Sofia and her incredible team. Though this restaurant sadly closed in October 2023, it remains an important part of our story. In January 2021, Sofia took over the Skewer House, and the following year, in partnership with her husband Pedro, opened the Corner Club — a vibrant Brunch & Dessert Parlour on Bath Place. At Corner Club, you’ll find our beloved cheesecakes, Nobrega’s homemade cronuts, and their signature Cookie Pie. You’ll also spot a few familiar nods to our family restaurants — the Skewer House Bagel and Nobregas Bagel among other sweet and savory delights.

What is an Espetada?

Espetada roughly translates to "skewer" or "sword" — and on the island of Madeira, they're more than just a meal; they’re a tradition. At Skewer House, we bring that tradition to life with long metal skewers of flame-grilled meat, served hanging from custom-made iron trees at the center of your table. Each skewer is infused with rich, smoky flavors and seasoned with generations of family knowledge. As a proud family-run restaurant, our recipes are passed down through the generations, making our Espetadas truly unique — a heartfelt tribute to our Madeiran heritage, served with warmth and authenticity.


Madeira: Island of Fire, Water & Flavor

Madeira is a small Portuguese island in the Atlantic Ocean, just off the coast of Morocco and north of the Canary Islands. Known for its lush landscapes and year-round mild weather, it’s home to the world’s largest laurel forest and an abundance of exotic flora.

Its name means “wood” — a nod to its dense forests. Though the island faces natural extremes like fires and floods, it always bounces back with vibrant culture, rich traditions, and incredible food.

It’s no surprise Madeira has been voted Europe’s Best Island several times.
